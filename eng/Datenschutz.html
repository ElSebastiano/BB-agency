Notice of Data Subject Rights


According to Article 15 of the GDPR, you have the right to request free information about whether personal data about yourself is being processed and, if so, what personal data is being processed. In general, you can request a copy of the data unless there is a legal exception. In this context, you can also request information about the specific circumstances and the expected duration of the processing. Based on your knowledge of the processing of your data, the following additional rights apply:
• If the data is incomplete or incorrect, you have the right to have it corrected under Article 16 of the GDPR.
• According to Article 17 of the GDPR, you have the right to request the deletion of your personal data if one of the deletion reasons mentioned therein applies, and there are no retention obligations, for example.
• In the cases mentioned in Article 18 of the GDPR, you can request the restriction of the processing of personal data.
• If personal data is processed based on consent or a contract, you can request the transfer of your personal data to another provider under Article 20 of the GDPR. Instead of transfer, you can also request the provision of your data in a common format.
• Due to a special personal situation and resulting hardship in the processing, you can object to an otherwise lawful processing under Article 21 of the GDPR. Pending a decision on the objection, you can request a restriction of the processing under Article 18 of the GDPR.
• Article 22 of the GDPR protects data subjects from individual automated decisions, including profiling.
